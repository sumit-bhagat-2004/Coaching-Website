WELCOME

Navodaya Vidyalayas draw their students strength from talented children, selected on the basis of a merit test, called the Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya Selection Test, designed, developed and conducted initially by NCERT and now by CBSE. The test is held annually on an All India basis and at block and district levels. The test is objective, class neutral and is so designed as to ensure that rural children are not at a disadvantage while attempting it. Know More

Eligibility Criteria

  • District Specific Admission: Candidates must study Class V in the district where they are seeking admission.
  • Age Requirements: Candidates should be born between May 1, 2013, and July 31, 2015.
  • Educational Requirements: Must have studied Classes III, IV, and V from a government or recognized school.
  • Rural and Urban Quota: At least 75% of seats are for rural candidates. The remaining seats are for both urban and rural candidates.

Salient Features

  • Free Education: Quality education with free boarding and lodging.
  • Reservation: Minimum one-third of seats reserved for girls. Reservation for Divyang (disabled) children as per GOI norms.
  • Aadhaar Requirement: Aadhaar number necessary for admission.

Benefits of JNVST

  • Holistic Education: Emphasis on overall development including academics, sports, and extracurricular activities.
  • Equal Opportunities: Promotes education among rural and underserved communities.
  • Free Resources: Access to textbooks, uniforms, and other educational materials at no cost.

Application Process

  • Online Application: Candidates can apply online. Help desks available at JNVs for assistance.
  • Document Submission: Details about required documents: proof of date of birth, eligibility, residence, Aadhaar card, and certificates.
